Three Approaches to Narrative Enrichment

Same goal — richer, more specific world — different strategies for getting there

A

Lore Layer — Named Characters & Places as Data

Strategy: Create a structured "lore registry" of named characters, places, products, and treaties. Rewrite existing event headlines to reference these entities contextually. Events pull from the registry based on world state.

Example: Instead of "Trade war tariffs escalate" → "Sen. Roy Lassiter (F-SC) rams Farsistan Reciprocal Tariff Act through Commerce Committee over Minority Leader Okafor's objection; Hang Seng futures drop 3%"

Scope: ~40-60 named entities across the 4 pillars. Headline templates with entity substitution. World-state-aware selection.

Tradeoff: Most systematic. Ensures consistency (same senator doesn't appear in contradictory roles). But templated headlines can feel formulaic if not carefully written.

B

Deep Rewrite — Hand-Craft Every Headline

Strategy: Manually rewrite every event's headline, context text, and toast to reference specific people, products, and places. Each event is a bespoke piece of micro-fiction. New world-state fields track character arcs.

Example: The same tariff event becomes a hand-written narrative: "Roy Lassiter holds the Senate floor for nine hours reading soybean import statistics. By midnight, the Farsistan Reciprocal Tariff Act passes 52-48. Lassiter's victory lap on The Sentinel draws 14 million viewers."

Scope: Touch every existing event. Rewrite ~277 toast headlines + ~30 popup contexts. Add character tracking to world state.

Tradeoff: Highest quality per-event. But massive writing effort, and adding new events later requires matching the voice. Risk of inconsistency across the large pool.

C

Hybrid — Registry + Targeted Rewrites

Strategy: Build a lightweight lore registry for consistency, then hand-rewrite the ~80-100 most impactful events (all popups, all major/moderate toasts, all compound triggers). Minor/filler events get lighter enrichment via the registry. New event chains for the 4 pillars use the registry from day one.

Example: Major events get the full bespoke treatment. Minor events get enriched headlines like "Lassiter tells The Sentinel he has 'the votes' for the tariff bill" — specific but shorter.

Scope: Lore registry (~40-60 entities). Deep rewrite of ~80-100 key events. Light enrichment of remaining ~180. New chains for products, geopolitics, media.

Tradeoff: Best balance of quality and feasibility. Registry prevents contradictions. Bespoke writing where it matters most. But two tiers of quality could feel uneven if the seam is visible.

My Recommendation: C (Hybrid)

A pure registry approach (A) risks feeling procedural. A pure hand-craft approach (B) is a huge amount of writing with consistency risks. The hybrid (C) gives you the registry as a single source of truth for "who is Roy Lassiter and what does he want" while putting the real writing effort into the events the player actually reads carefully — popups, superevents, compound triggers, and the epilogue.
