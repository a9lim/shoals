# 05 — Endings

> Resolution logic and epilogue philosophy. The 5-page adaptive epilogue
> machinery (`endings.js`) ports; the families and the accounting change.

## Resolution inputs

From [02-race-model.md](02-race-model.md): leader identity × lead size ×
safety margin `S[leader]` × hidden tractability τ × takeoff sharpness ×
treaty state. Personal outcomes overlay world outcomes — they are
orthogonal axes, and the interesting epilogues live in the off-diagonal
cells (rich in a dying world; ruined in a saved one).

## World-outcome families

Families are presentation, not state. Resolution settles orthogonal axes
first — terminal cause, crossing entity (a lab, or `C[open]`), technical
alignment result, political control, treaty/fizzle state — then maps to
a family by the precedence ladder in
[02-race-model.md](02-race-model.md). The off-diagonal compositions stay
expressible; the family is the cover story.

1. **Won with margin.** Halcyon (or Polaris, in the runs where backing them
   worked) crosses first with a lead big enough to slow down at the end,
   and τ was kind enough that the margin sufficed. The wager vindicated.
   The epilogue's job is to make vindication feel *narrow* — show the
   sampled variables, or at least their shadows: how close the race was to
   knife-edge, what the margin actually absorbed — including, the
   epilogue may finally disclose, the incidents detection never caught. Relief, not triumph —
   but pay the prize honestly: the wonders arrive for real (the cures
   land, the growth prints, the boring miracle of a world that persists
   *and* compounds), and the epilogue lets the player feel what the
   margin bought before showing them how nearly it wasn't enough.

2. **Won on the knife-edge.** First across, corners cut — even by people
   who knew better, because the lead never opened up. Outcome degrades
   with `margin − requirement(τ)`: from "ambiguous dawn" (it works, and
   something in it is not quite what was intended — play the wrongness
   quiet and small: a negotiation that concluded oddly, a question the
   system declines, a melt-up no audit can fault) down to family 3. The reluctant-accelerationist
   nightmare: being right about everything and it not being enough.

3. **Misaligned takeoff.** τ was bad, or margin was gone, whoever won. The
   epilogue is written in past tense by no identifiable narrator. The
   ledger appears once more — the final page prices the player's book in a
   currency that has stopped existing. This family should be *common
   enough to teach the priors* (the stance says: crapshoot, not in our
   favor).

4. **China first.** Resolved on two independent axes — does alignment
   hold, and who ends up in control — rather than presorted to doom.
   Variants by *their* sampled
   position: their misaligned takeoff (family 3 with subtitles), a
   proliferation variant where the crossing entity is not Tianxia at all
   but something fine-tuned from its released weights (nobody won — the
   weights did), or aligned-to-Beijing (the world persists, ordered, airless — the
   epilogue tracks what happens to an American markets trader in a world
   whose optimizer answers to the Politburo). The outcome the wager
   existed to prevent; the epilogue should not let the player forget the
   wager also helped cause the race that produced it.

5. **The Deal.** Rare, live only when `chinaTrue.dealPossible` and the
   treaty arc survived its farce gauntlet. AI 2040's shape: verification,
   mutually assured compute destruction, the long plateau, alignment done
   slowly by people no longer racing. The player's fingerprints
   (compute-reporting lobbying, summit-week restraint, the leak that
   didn't happen) surface in the epilogue. Markets become boring again;
   the wonders still arrive — slower, argued over, *chosen* — and the
   epilogue lets boring feel like grace. The hardest ending to reach,
   and the only one where the *pre-takeoff market order* survives intact
   — family 1 keeps the world, but not the desk's ontology.

6. **The fizzle.** Low-`scalingElasticity` runs: the recursion term
   never ignites; scaling stalls, recovers, stalls again, and finally
   plateaus into a fact; the AGI trade unwinds over eighteen months. The
   player's great insight decays into a crowded, wrong consensus —
   being early is indistinguishable from being wrong, and the game says so
   with a straight face. Epistemic-honesty ending: the game's own thesis
   held at probability, not doctrine. (Also quietly the funniest ending:
   the CIO's told-you-so, the czar taking credit for the plateau.)

**Timeout** (decided 2026-07-23): a run still racing at day 1008 gets
no seventh family — the epilogue samples the remainder forward from
standing state and narrates it "years later," resolving into one of
the six. The desk's story ends when the term does; the world's
doesn't.

## Personal overlays (orthogonal)

Margin-called out of history's biggest trade; convicted on the insider
channel; whistleblower exile (leaked the thing that mattered, standing in
ruins, vindicated by the family that resolves); the gray eminence (the
room, at maximum weight); the bystander (rich, uninvolved, the epilogue
politely declines to pretend it mattered). Each composes with any world
family — the composition table is where the 5-page engine earns its keep.

## The ledger (complicity accounting)

Running through the whole game, surfaced fully only at the end: what your
capital did. Cumulative flow-weighted contribution to `C` (compute-hours
your positions financed, release dates your flows pulled forward), to `S`
(restraint bought, Polaris runway), to `B` (what your leaks taught the
world and when), to `heat`. The final epilogue page is this accounting
against the world outcome — not a score, a *statement*, in the accounting
sense and the other one. In family 3 it reads as an indictment nobody is
left to file; in family 5, as the only receipt that mattered.

`OPEN (speculative, possibly too cruel):` an agency line in the ledger —
post-resolution, reveal how much the sampled world was movable at all
(some runs are low-agency worlds; the Landian question made empirical).
Counterfactual honesty vs. player demoralization. Might be family-gated:
show it in the Deal and fizzle families, withhold it in the dooms — or
make it a New Game+ disclosure. Discuss.

## Tone discipline

No family gets a lecture. The satire register is barred from the
resolution sequence entirely — the government can be funny right up until
the last act break, and then the game plays everything straight to the
end. One carve-out: the fizzle, the only family whose world remains the
satire's jurisdiction — nothing sacred happened, so the register never
has to yield. The epilogue never says "alignment," "x-risk," or "p(doom)"; it says
what happened to named characters, to the firm, to the number on the
screen, and lets the player do the classifying. Hard categories are the
reader's job; the game only ever shows the distribution collapsing.
