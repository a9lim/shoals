## Forks — all adopted 2026-07-23, per a9, with amendments

a9 adopted every recommendation below (the "recommend" notes stand as
rationale) and amended two: **superpersuasion is additionally an
incident class**, and — a9's own extension — **incidents themselves went
two-track** (occurrence vs. detection), generalized with capability
into the "two-track everywhere" principle in
[02-race-model.md](02-race-model.md). All items below are implemented
in the docs same-day; the entries are kept as the record of why.

✓ **Standing orders in Act III** (recommend: adopt — the pass's best
find). Tempo compression as more popups is notification spam, not the
loss of decision-relevance. Instead: before R4 the game pushes the
player to author standing orders, risk limits, leak policies, delegated
rules; during recursion those precommitments execute while manual
interventions become scarce/laggy. The felt sense of takeoff = your own
agency moving into machinery you configured earlier. Engine cost is
real but bounded (an order-rules layer over existing order machinery).

✓ **Two-track capability: internal vs. released** (recommend: adopt,
lightweight). AI 2027's decisive gap — what the lab has vs. what the
world sees — currently lives only in flavor (Fixedpoint via insider
channel). Make it state: `C_internal[lab]` vs. `C_released[lab]`, with
internal deployment driving the recursion term and releases being
*choices* (heat, revenue, evidence). Codex's full four-stage pipeline is
overkill; two tracks buy most of the fantasy.

✓ **Alignment-evidence ladder before the room** (recommend: adopt as
arc). Currently the player has no *technical* evidence stream — `S` is
an armor stat and the room's choice is ideological, not epistemic. Add
an evidence beat-family (eval anomalies, hidden-reasoning findings,
control-experiment results, disputed replications) with sampled
reliability, τ-correlated but noisy — the alignment-side twin of the
incident ladder. The room then *continues* an evidence trail rather
than introducing the dilemma cold. Content on existing rails; big
thematic payoff.

✓ **`controlRegime` state** (recommend: adopt). Government takeover is
currently a lobbying menu item. Make it an enum — private → supervised →
mobilized → nationalized — that reshapes release visibility, HCN's very
existence (delisting/conversion!), Consensus availability, and the
player's access. The nationalization *trade* (what happens to your book
when the state absorbs the issuer) is desk-fantasy gold and currently
unspecified — it's also half of the market-integrity spec queued in 07.

✓ **Security levels** (recommend: adopt, slim). Replace scalar security
posture with 3–4 discrete levels (RAND-flavored: startup → corporate →
compartmentalized → state-grade), each with costs, research friction,
and observable tells. Theft hazard keys off level vs. attacker effort.
Slim version only — no compromise-state simulation.

✓ **Fizzle: boolean → scaling elasticity** (recommend: adopt —
soft-everywhere applied to ourselves). `fizzleWorld: bool` is a hard
category; a hidden continuous elasticity that degrades training
returns/cadence produces noisy stalls and recoveries, keeps the fizzle
tradeable rather than a gotcha, and merges cleanly with
`takeoffSharpness` at the low end.

✓ **Bounded influence budget** (recommend: adopt as a tuning principle,
one line in 02). "A seat, not a steering wheel" is currently
aspiration; the coupling map lets the player move every dial. Cap the
player's total contribution to any outcome threshold at a bounded
minority of log-odds; the rest comes from NPC actors acting
independently. Keeps the ledger meaningful without making the player
the race's secret protagonist.

✓ **Superpersuasion mechanics** (recommend: partial). The ghostwritten
world is currently prose texture. Minimal mechanization: past a rung,
targeted campaigns exist as events that move `B`/`F`/faction standings
*adversarially* (someone's model is optimizing the feed), and
insider-channel source trust becomes attackable. Codex's full
provenance/verification-budget economy is a whole second game — cut.

## Deliberate cuts (recorded so they read as choices, not oversights)

- **Endogenous macro sim** (automated labor share, wage collapse,
  fiscal capacity as linked stocks): out of scope for this chassis. The
  agent economy stays legible through events, sector prints, and the
  rates channel. Revisit only if the wonders track feels weightless in
  play.
- **Compute stock-flow ledger** (per-generation, per-jurisdiction
  accounting): compute stays a per-lab scalar stock moved by events
  (Cambria allocation, export controls, theft integration,
  mobilization). The compute-futures curve is its market shadow; that's
  the desk's view anyway.
- **Doom-shorts never settle** — confirmed as thematic feature, with
  the proviso (per the ambition pass) that terminal wealth in doom
  families is priced by the epilogue as what it is: a claim on a
  counterparty that no longer exists.
